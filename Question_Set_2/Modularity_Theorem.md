# Modularity Theorem

## The Question
For many years mathematicians suspected that every elliptic curve defined over the rational numbers is closely connected to a modular form.

At first glance these objects come from very different areas of mathematics.

- Elliptic curves arise in algebraic geometry and number theory.
- Modular forms arise in complex analysis and the theory of symmetries.

The challenge was to prove that these worlds are fundamentally linked.

## Why It Was Important
The theorem became one of the central problems in modern number theory.

Its importance increased dramatically after Gerhard Frey, Jean-Pierre Serre, and Kenneth Ribet showed that a proof would imply Fermat's Last Theorem.

As a result, proving modularity became one of the most important goals in mathematics.

## How It Was Solved
Andrew Wiles proved a major semistable case in the 1990s.

After a gap was discovered, Wiles and Richard Taylor completed the argument.

Subsequent work by Christophe Breuil, Brian Conrad, Fred Diamond, and Richard Taylor extended the result to all elliptic curves over the rationals.

## Final Result
Every elliptic curve over the rational numbers is modular.

This result forms one of the foundations of modern arithmetic geometry.
