# Classification of Finite Simple Groups

## The Question
Prime numbers are often described as the building blocks of the integers.

In group theory, finite simple groups play a similar role. Every finite group can be assembled from simple groups.

The question was:

Can all finite simple groups be completely classified?

## Why It Was Important
A positive answer would provide a complete list of the fundamental building blocks from which all finite symmetry structures can be constructed.

This became one of the largest projects in twentieth-century mathematics.

## How It Was Solved
The proof was not the work of a single mathematician.

Instead, hundreds of researchers contributed over several decades.

The final classification shows that every finite simple group belongs to one of several infinite families or one of the 26 sporadic groups.

The proof is spread across thousands of pages and many journal articles.

## Final Result
Every finite simple group belongs to one of:

- Cyclic groups of prime order
- Alternating groups
- Groups of Lie type
- One of the 26 sporadic simple groups

## Historical Importance
This is widely regarded as one of the largest mathematical proofs ever completed.
