# Boolean Pythagorean Triples Problem

## The Question
Can the positive integers be colored using two colors so that no Pythagorean triple

(a,b,c)

with

a² + b² = c²

is monochromatic?

The question combines Ramsey theory, number theory, and combinatorics.

## Why It Was Important
The problem appears deceptively simple but quickly becomes computationally enormous.

Traditional mathematical techniques were unable to settle the problem.

## How It Was Solved
In 2016, Marijn Heule, Oliver Kullmann, and Victor Marek solved the problem using SAT solvers.

The computation generated one of the largest mathematical proofs ever produced.

The proof file was hundreds of terabytes before compression and verification.

## Final Result
The integers from 1 to 7824 can be colored with two colors while avoiding monochromatic Pythagorean triples.

This becomes impossible for the integers from 1 to 7825.

## Original Paper

https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.00723

## Why It Matters Historically
This is one of the clearest examples of a major theorem established through large-scale automated reasoning.
