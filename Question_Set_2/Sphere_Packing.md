# Sphere Packing in Dimensions 8 and 24

## The Question
Given identical spheres, what is the densest possible way to arrange them without overlap?

The problem is easy to visualize in three dimensions, but becomes extraordinarily difficult in higher dimensions.

For decades mathematicians searched for the optimal arrangements in dimensions 8 and 24.

## Why It Was Important
Sphere packing is connected to coding theory, cryptography, information transmission, discrete geometry, and optimization.

The E8 lattice and the Leech lattice had long been suspected to be optimal, but nobody could prove it.

## How It Was Solved
In 2016, Maryna Viazovska produced a remarkable proof showing that the E8 lattice gives the densest sphere packing in dimension 8.

Soon afterward, Viazovska and collaborators extended the ideas to dimension 24 and proved the optimality of the Leech lattice.

The proof introduced unexpected connections between Fourier analysis, modular forms, and discrete geometry.

## Final Result
- E8 lattice is optimal in dimension 8.
- Leech lattice is optimal in dimension 24.
