# Robbins Conjecture

## The Question
David Robbins studied alternating sign matrices and observed several remarkable counting patterns.

He conjectured an exact formula for the number of alternating sign matrices of size n.

An alternating sign matrix is a square matrix whose entries are -1, 0, or 1 and satisfy special row and column conditions.

## Why It Was Important
The conjecture linked combinatorics, statistical mechanics, and algebra.

Although the formula looked elegant, proving it turned out to be surprisingly difficult.

## How It Was Solved
The conjecture was eventually proved by Doron Zeilberger in 1996.

His proof combined sophisticated combinatorial arguments with identities related to determinant evaluations.

Additional proofs and simplifications appeared later.

## Final Result
The exact enumeration formula proposed by Robbins is correct.
