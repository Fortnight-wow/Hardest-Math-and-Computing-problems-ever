# Poincaré Conjecture

## The Question
Henri Poincaré asked in 1904 whether every simply connected, closed 3-manifold is topologically equivalent to the 3-sphere.

In simple terms, if a three-dimensional space has no holes and is finite in extent, must it really be a distorted version of an ordinary 3-sphere?

For decades this became one of the central problems in topology.

## Why It Was Important
The conjecture sits at the heart of geometric topology.

A solution would provide a deep understanding of how three-dimensional spaces can be classified.

Many special cases were solved, but the full problem resisted progress for nearly a century.

## How It Was Solved
Richard Hamilton developed the Ricci Flow program, a method that continuously smooths the geometry of a manifold.

The remaining obstacle was understanding singularities that form during the flow.

In 2002 and 2003, Grigori Perelman introduced new ideas that controlled these singularities and completed Hamilton's program.

His work established not only the Poincaré Conjecture but also the more general Geometrization Conjecture.

## Final Result
Every closed, simply connected 3-manifold is homeomorphic to the 3-sphere.
