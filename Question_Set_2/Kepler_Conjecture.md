# Kepler Conjecture

## The Question
Johannes Kepler proposed in 1611 that the familiar arrangement used by grocers stacking oranges is the densest possible packing of identical spheres.

The challenge was to prove that no other arrangement can achieve a higher density.

## Why It Was Important
Sphere packing appears in geometry, coding theory, crystallography, and information theory.

Although the statement seems simple, proving it required analyzing an enormous number of geometric possibilities.

## How It Was Solved
Thomas Hales completed a proof in the late 1990s using a combination of geometric arguments and extensive computer verification.

Because of the complexity of the proof, the Flyspeck Project was later launched to formally verify every step using proof assistants.

## Final Result
The face-centered cubic packing and equivalent packings achieve the maximum possible density.
