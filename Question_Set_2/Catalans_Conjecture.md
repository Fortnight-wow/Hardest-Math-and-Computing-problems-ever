# Catalan's Conjecture

## The Question
Proposed by Eugène Catalan in 1844.

The conjecture states that the only consecutive perfect powers are:

8 = 2^3
9 = 3^2

Equivalently, the equation

x^a - y^b = 1

has exactly one solution in positive integers with exponents greater than 1.

## Why It Was Important
The problem belongs to Diophantine equations, a field concerned with integer solutions.

Although the statement is simple, it resisted proof for more than 150 years.

## How It Was Solved
Preda Mihăilescu proved the conjecture in 2002.

The proof relied on deep properties of cyclotomic fields and algebraic number theory rather than elementary techniques.

## Final Result
The only pair of consecutive perfect powers is:

8 and 9.
