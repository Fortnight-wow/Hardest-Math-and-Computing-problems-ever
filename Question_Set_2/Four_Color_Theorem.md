# Four Color Theorem

## The Question
Can every planar map be colored using at most four colors so that neighboring regions always receive different colors?

The question was first posed in 1852 and became one of the most famous problems in graph theory.

## Why It Was Important
The statement is easy to understand, yet every attempted proof failed for more than a century.

It became a testing ground for new ideas in combinatorics and graph theory.

## How It Was Solved
Kenneth Appel and Wolfgang Haken announced a proof in 1976.

Their approach reduced the problem to checking a very large collection of unavoidable configurations.

The verification required extensive computer calculations.

This made it one of the first major theorems whose proof depended heavily on computer assistance.

## Final Result
Every planar graph can be colored with at most four colors.
