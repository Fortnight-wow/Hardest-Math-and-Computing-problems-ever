# AI-Assisted Mathematics

## Overview

For most of mathematical history, major breakthroughs were produced entirely by human researchers. Over the last few decades, computers have become increasingly important in both discovering and verifying mathematical results.

Today, artificial intelligence, automated theorem provers, SAT solvers, and formal verification systems are beginning to contribute to mathematical research.

This file highlights some of the most important AI-assisted and computer-assisted milestones.

---

## 1. Four Color Theorem (1976)

### Why It Matters
The first famous theorem whose proof relied heavily on computer verification.

### Researchers
- Kenneth Appel
- Wolfgang Haken

### Contribution of Computers
Thousands of unavoidable configurations were checked computationally.

### Reference
https://www.ams.org/journals/bull/1977-82-05/S0002-9904-1977-14398-6/

---

## 2. Kepler Conjecture and Flyspeck

### Why It Matters
One of the largest formal verification projects ever completed.

### Researchers
- Thomas Hales

### Contribution of Computers
Proof assistants formally verified every step of the proof.

### References
https://annals.math.princeton.edu/2005/162-3/p05

https://arxiv.org/abs/1501.02155

## 3. Boolean Pythagorean Triples Problem

### Why It Matters
A landmark result obtained using SAT solvers.

### Researchers
- Marijn Heule
- Oliver Kullmann
- Victor Marek

### Contribution of AI/Automation
Automated reasoning generated and verified an enormous proof.

### Reference
https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.00723

## Future Directions

Researchers are exploring:

- AI-generated conjectures
- Automated theorem proving
- Formal proof verification
- Human-AI mathematical collaboration
- Machine-assisted discovery in number theory and geometry

The most likely future is not AI replacing mathematicians, but mathematicians and AI systems working together on problems that are currently beyond either working alone.
