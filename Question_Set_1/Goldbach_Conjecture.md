# Goldbach Conjecture

## The Question

In 1742, Christian Goldbach wrote a letter to Leonhard Euler suggesting a remarkably simple statement:

> Every even integer greater than 2 can be written as the sum of two prime numbers.

Examples:

- 4 = 2 + 2
- 10 = 5 + 5
- 28 = 11 + 17
- 100 = 47 + 53

Despite its simplicity, nobody has been able to prove it.

## Why It Matters

The conjecture sits at the center of additive number theory.

It asks how prime numbers combine to form other integers and is closely connected to our understanding of the distribution of primes.

## Progress So Far

The conjecture has been checked by computers for enormous ranges of integers.

No counterexample has ever been found.

Several weaker versions have been proved.

Most notably:

- Vinogradov proved that sufficiently large odd integers can be written as the sum of three primes.
- Harald Helfgott completed the proof of the weak Goldbach conjecture in 2013.
