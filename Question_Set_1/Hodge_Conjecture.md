# Hodge Conjecture

## The Question

Algebraic geometry studies shapes defined by polynomial equations.

Topology studies the global structure of spaces.

The Hodge Conjecture proposes a deep connection between these two viewpoints.

In rough terms, it asks whether certain topological features of a projective algebraic variety can always be explained using algebraic cycles.

## Why It Matters

The conjecture lies at the heart of modern algebraic geometry.

A proof would reveal profound connections between geometry, topology, and complex analysis.

Many important results in arithmetic geometry and mathematical physics are related to ideas originating from Hodge theory.

## Historical Background

The conjecture was formulated by W. V. D. Hodge during the twentieth century.

Many special cases have been solved, but the general problem remains open.

## Prize

The Hodge Conjecture is one of the Clay Mathematics Institute Millennium Prize Problems.

Prize amount: $1,000,000.
