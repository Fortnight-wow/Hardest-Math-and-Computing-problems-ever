# Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap

## The Question

Quantum Yang-Mills theory forms the mathematical foundation of the strong nuclear force.

Physicists use Yang-Mills theories successfully every day, yet a complete mathematical foundation remains missing.

The challenge is to show that a quantum Yang-Mills theory exists mathematically and possesses a positive mass gap.

In practical terms, there should be a strictly positive minimum energy required to create an excitation.

## Why It Matters

The Standard Model of particle physics relies heavily on Yang-Mills theories.

A rigorous solution would strengthen the mathematical foundations of modern physics.

## Historical Background

Yang-Mills theory was introduced by Chen Ning Yang and Robert Mills in 1954.

It later became one of the cornerstones of quantum field theory.

Despite its success in experiments, the required mathematical proof remains elusive.
