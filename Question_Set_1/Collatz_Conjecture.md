# Collatz Conjecture

## The Question

Take any positive integer n.

- If n is even, divide it by 2.
- If n is odd, replace it with 3n + 1.

Repeat the process indefinitely.

The Collatz Conjecture states that every positive integer eventually reaches 1.

Example:

10 → 5 → 16 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1

Despite being easy enough to explain in a few seconds, nobody knows how to prove that the process always reaches 1.

## Why It Matters

The problem demonstrates how simple deterministic rules can generate surprisingly complicated behavior.

It is often cited as an example of the limits of current mathematical techniques.

## Progress So Far

Computers have verified the conjecture for enormous ranges of integers.

In 2019, Terence Tao proved that almost all Collatz orbits eventually become much smaller than their starting values, a major breakthrough though not a complete proof.
