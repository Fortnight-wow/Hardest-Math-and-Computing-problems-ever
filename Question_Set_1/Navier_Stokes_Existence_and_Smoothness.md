# Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness

## The Question

The Navier-Stokes equations describe the motion of fluids such as water and air.

In three dimensions, the equations are

∂u/∂t + (u·∇)u = -∇p + νΔu

along with the incompressibility condition.

The question is:

Given smooth initial conditions, do smooth solutions always exist for all future time?

Or can singularities develop?

## Why It Matters

These equations form the mathematical foundation of fluid dynamics.

They are used throughout engineering, aerospace, meteorology, oceanography, and physics.

A proof would significantly improve our understanding of turbulence.

## What Makes It Difficult

The nonlinear term allows energy to move between scales in complicated ways.

Mathematicians do not currently know whether this process can create infinite quantities in finite time.

## Prize

This is one of the Clay Mathematics Institute Millennium Prize Problems.

A correct solution carries a prize of $1,000,000.
