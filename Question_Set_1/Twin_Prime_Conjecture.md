# Twin Prime Conjecture

## The Question

Twin primes are pairs of prime numbers that differ by exactly two.

Examples:

- (3, 5)
- (5, 7)
- (11, 13)
- (17, 19)

The conjecture asks:

> Are there infinitely many twin primes?

## Why It Matters

Prime numbers become less common as numbers grow larger.

The Twin Prime Conjecture asks whether close pairs of primes continue to appear forever.

The problem is deeply connected to our understanding of prime distributions.

## Major Breakthroughs

For many years no significant progress was made.

In 2013, Yitang Zhang proved that infinitely many prime pairs exist whose difference is less than 70 million.

This was a historic breakthrough.

Subsequent work by James Maynard, Terence Tao, and the Polymath Project dramatically reduced the bound.

Although the gap has been reduced substantially, nobody has yet reached a gap of exactly 2.
