# Millennium Prize Problems

In 2000, the Clay Mathematics Institute announced seven major unsolved problems and offered a prize of $1,000,000 for each solution.

These problems represent some of the deepest questions in modern mathematics.

## The Seven Problems

### Solved

1. Poincaré Conjecture
   - Solved by Grigori Perelman (2002–2003)

### Still Open

2. Riemann Hypothesis
3. P versus NP
4. Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness
5. Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap
6. Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture
7. Hodge Conjecture

## Official Source

https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems/

## Historical Note

Perelman declined both the Fields Medal and the Millennium Prize associated with the Poincaré Conjecture.
